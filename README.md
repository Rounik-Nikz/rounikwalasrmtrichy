### **1) Unit Conversions**
i) \( 1000 \, \text{cm/s to ft/min} \):
\[
\text{Conversion factors:} \, 1 \, \text{cm} = 0.0328084 \, \text{ft}, \, 1 \, \text{s} = 1/60 \, \text{min}
\]
\[
1000 \, \text{cm/s} = 1000 \times 0.0328084 \times 60 = 1968.5 \, \text{ft/min}
\]

ii) \( 2000 \, \text{mm Hg to atm} \):
\[
\text{Conversion factor:} \, 1 \, \text{atm} = 760 \, \text{mm Hg}
\]
\[
2000 \, \text{mm Hg} = \frac{2000}{760} = 2.632 \, \text{atm}
\]

iii) \( 5 \, \text{g/cm}^3 \, \text{to} \, \text{lb/ft}^3 \):
\[
\text{Conversion factors:} \, 1 \, \text{g/cm}^3 = 62.428 \, \text{lb/ft}^3
\]
\[
5 \, \text{g/cm}^3 = 5 \times 62.428 = 312.14 \, \text{lb/ft}^3
\]

iv) \( 100 \, \text{lb/in}^2 \, \text{to} \, \text{N/m}^2 \):
\[
\text{Conversion factors:} \, 1 \, \text{lb/in}^2 = 6894.76 \, \text{N/m}^2
\]
\[
100 \, \text{lb/in}^2 = 100 \times 6894.76 = 689,476 \, \text{N/m}^2
\]

v) \( 100 \, \text{Cal/gmol K to J/kmol K} \):
\[
\text{Conversion factor:} \, 1 \, \text{Cal/gmol K} = 4184 \, \text{J/kmol K}
\]
\[
100 \, \text{Cal/gmol K} = 100 \times 4184 = 418,400 \, \text{J/kmol K}
\]

vi) \( 50 \, \text{Btu/h ft}^2 \degree F \, \text{to W/mK} \):
\[
\text{Conversion factor:} \, 1 \, \text{Btu/h ft}^2 \degree F = 5.678 \, \text{W/mK}
\]
\[
50 \, \text{Btu/h ft}^2 \degree F = 50 \times 5.678 = 283.9 \, \text{W/mK}
\]

### **2) Temperature Conversion**
Given \( T = 70 \degree C \):
- \( T(K) = T(\degree C) + 273.15 = 70 + 273.15 = 343.15 \, K \)
- \( T(\degree F) = \frac{9}{5} T(\degree C) + 32 = \frac{9}{5} \times 70 + 32 = 158 \, \degree F \)
- \( T(R) = T(\degree F) + 459.67 = 158 + 459.67 = 617.67 \, \degree R \)

### **3) Absolute Pressure in Different Units**
Vacuum pressure = \( 450 \, \text{torr} \):
\[
\text{Absolute pressure:} \, P_{\text{abs}} = 760 - 450 = 310 \, \text{torr}
\]
Convert \( P_{\text{abs}} \) to:
- \( \text{kPa}: 1 \, \text{torr} = 0.133322 \, \text{kPa} \Rightarrow P = 310 \times 0.133322 = 41.33 \, \text{kPa} \)
- \( \text{bar}: 1 \, \text{bar} = 100 \, \text{kPa} \Rightarrow P = \frac{41.33}{100} = 0.4133 \, \text{bar} \)
- \( \text{kg/cm}^2 \): \( 1 \, \text{kg/cm}^2 = 98.0665 \, \text{kPa} \Rightarrow P = \frac{41.33}{98.0665} = 0.421 \, \text{kg/cm}^2 \)
- \( \text{psia}: 1 \, \text{torr} = 0.019337 \, \text{psia} \Rightarrow P = 310 \times 0.019337 = 6.0 \, \text{psia} \)

### **4) Absolute Pressure in Different Units**
Given \( P_{\text{gauge}} = 25 \, \text{psi} \), \( P_{\text{abs}} = P_{\text{gauge}} + 14.7 \, \text{psi} \):
- \( P_{\text{abs}} = 25 + 14.7 = 39.7 \, \text{psi} \)
Convert \( P_{\text{abs}} \):
- \( \text{atm}: 1 \, \text{atm} = 14.7 \, \text{psi} \Rightarrow P = \frac{39.7}{14.7} = 2.7 \, \text{atm} \)
- \( \text{N/m}^2 \): \( 1 \, \text{psi} = 6894.76 \, \text{N/m}^2 \Rightarrow P = 39.7 \times 6894.76 = 2.73 \times 10^5 \, \text{N/m}^2 \)
